12th International Conference

Advancements and Futuristic Trends in Mechanical and Materials Engineering

AFTMME 2026 | 19-20 November, 2026 | Guru Nanak Dev Engineering College, Ludhiana

The inaugural AFTMME conference was started in 2010 with the goal of bringing together mechanical and materials engineers under a common umbrella to bring academicians, researchers, practising engineers, industrialists and start-up professionals together for sharing their ideas and experiences and disseminating knowledge.

Mechanical and Materials Engineering have long had a symbiotic relationship in this space. The conference aims to share knowledge and findings of researchers, academicians and people from industries about materials and manufacturing for sustainable growth.

Participants are welcome to present their ideas and current findings on materials and manufacturing for a greener future. The conference will also witness prominent people of their respected fields as keynote speakers.

Publication and Indexing Opportunities

Selected peer-reviewed papers from AFTMME 2026 will be considered for proposed proceedings, edited volume, or journal special issues, subject to approval by the concerned publisher or journal. The organizing committee is preparing a proposal for Springer Lecture Notes in Mechanical Engineering and exploring special issue collaborations with reputed journals in mechanical engineering, materials engineering, smart manufacturing, and sustainable engineering systems.

Proposed Springer Proceedings

The organizing committee intends to submit a proposal for selected peer-reviewed papers from AFTMME 2026 to be considered for publication in Springer’s Lecture Notes in Mechanical Engineering book series. Final acceptance of the proceedings proposal and individual papers will remain subject to Springer’s editorial evaluation, peer-review standards, formatting requirements, and publication policies.

About Ludhiana

Ludhiana, often called the Manchester of India, is the largest city in Punjab and a major industrial hub of northern India. Known for textiles, hosiery, manufacturing, and entrepreneurship, the city blends Punjabi warmth with industrial energy.

Ludhiana, often called the Manchester of India, is the largest city in Punjab and a major industrial hub of northern India. Known for its vibrant textile industry, hosiery exports, and entrepreneurial spirit, Ludhiana blends tradition with progress. Beyond its economic prowess, the city is rich in Punjabi culture — from lively folk dances and sumptuous cuisine to warm hospitality. The Sutlej River runs along its northern edge, offering a serene contrast to the city’s bustling life. Whether you’re walking through old bazaars, exploring Gurudwaras, or enjoying a glass of lassi at a roadside dhaba, Ludhiana promises an experience full of heart and energy.

Destinations Around Ludhiana

  • Gurudwara Dukh Niwaran Sahib - A sacred Sikh shrine known for its peaceful atmosphere and historical importance.
  • Punjab Agricultural University Museum - A glimpse into Punjab’s rural heritage, traditional crafts, and agricultural evolution.
  • Nehru Rose Garden - A sprawling urban park home to over 1,600 varieties of roses, ideal for picnics and evening strolls.
  • Phillaur Fort - A historical fort built by Maharaja Ranjit Singh, showcasing Sikh military architecture and British influences.
  • Rakh Bagh Park - A beloved recreational spot featuring lush greenery, a toy train, and spaces for families to unwind.
  • Hardy’s World - Punjab’s famous amusement and water park, perfect for thrill-seekers and family fun.
